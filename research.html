§ I — Overview
Research Mission

Human-Centered
Automation

Our research aims to reimagine automation in construction by integrating robotics, artificial intelligence, physiological sensing, and immersive simulation to create safer job sites, improve worker performance, and enable the efficient and sustainable delivery of infrastructure.

We have contributed to a diverse portfolio of projects: developing risk assessment frameworks for wearable robots, prototyping low-cost robotic systems for hazard detection, creating real-time physiological monitoring pipelines, and designing immersive virtual environments for robotics education and workforce training.

We plan to build a research program that advances scalable, intelligent, and human-centered automation for construction — spanning three current thrusts and five future frontiers.

§ II — Current Thrusts
Active Research

Three Core Thrusts

I
Robots in Construction Workflows
Integrating autonomous and semi-autonomous robotic systems into real-world construction processes. We develop frameworks for safe human-robot collaboration, adaptive task planning, and field-deployable low-cost robotic platforms that work effectively in unstructured, hazardous job-site environments. Our goal is to reduce worker exposure to danger while preserving the human judgment and craft that construction demands.
II
Workplace Health & Safety
Enhancing worker well-being through physiological sensing and AI-driven analytics. We design real-time monitoring pipelines using wearable sensors to detect fatigue, cognitive overload, thermal stress, and biomechanical risk exposure — enabling proactive safety interventions before incidents occur. This thrust bridges occupational health science with embedded computing and machine learning.
III
Immersive & AI-Enhanced Education
Modernizing engineering education through virtual reality environments, immersive simulation, and intelligent learning platforms. We train tomorrow's construction workforce using XR-based tools that make complex robotics and automation concepts accessible, engaging, and deeply experiential. This thrust also explores how physiological feedback can personalize learning in real time.
§ III — Future Directions
Looking Forward

Five Research Frontiers

01
Adaptive Robots for Collaborative Autonomy
Developing robots capable of adaptive decision-making during active construction operations — enabling genuine human-robot collaboration on complex, dynamic tasks in cluttered and safety-critical environments. This direction targets real-time replanning, shared autonomy, and trust-calibrated cooperation between human workers and robotic systems.
Robotics
02
Low-Cost Robotic Platforms for Hazard Detection
Designing accessible robotic platforms — including drones, hexapods, and humanoid robots — tailored for hazard detection and task automation in resource-constrained construction settings globally. The goal is democratizing intelligent safety: making autonomous inspection and monitoring affordable for projects at any scale.
Platforms
03
3D Concrete Printing with Integrated Sensing
Advancing additive construction through the fusion of real-time material sensing and robotic control — enabling adaptive, high-fidelity concrete printing that responds dynamically to material rheology, environmental conditions, and structural feedback. This work connects advanced manufacturing, materials science, and robotics.
Manufacturing
04
Supervisory Cyber-Physical-Human Systems
Creating supervisory frameworks that integrate physical sensing, digital models, and human performance data for real-time risk evaluation and proactive, personalized safety feedback on live job sites. This direction extends current physiological monitoring toward comprehensive site-level situational awareness — a digital twin of human health on construction sites.
Safety
05
Generative AI–Powered Learning Platforms
Building next-generation educational ecosystems that merge immersive simulation, physiological sensing, and intelligent tutoring to deliver personalized, adaptive learning experiences for the construction workforce of the future. These platforms will respond in real time to learner state, adjusting difficulty, pacing, and content using AI — bridging education technology and human-computer interaction.
Education
